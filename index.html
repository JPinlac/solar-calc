Stage Your Investment — Nothing Gets Thrown Away

The recommended path is built around Victron + Docan Panda. Each stage is independently useful and Stage 1 components are still in service in Stage 3. You don't have to commit to a full system on day one.

Battery Arbitrage

Side Benefits Beyond the Savings

The dollar-payback case stands on its own. These are the reasons people actually install systems — and they don't show up on the calculator.

🔌

Whole-Home Backup

The same battery that does rate arbitrage all day is your generator when DTE goes down. A 32 kWh Panda runs a typical home ~1.5 days off-grid. Silent, no fuel, no maintenance, no carbon monoxide.

📈

Hedge Against Rate Hikes

DTE rates climbed ~3.4% per year for the last decade and are accelerating. Every year the arbitrage spread grows and your battery becomes more valuable. See the 2026–2036 projections.

🚗

EV-Ready From Day One

Stage 1 puts you on D1.13. Any EV you buy from that point forward charges at 11.74¢/kWh — the equivalent of ~3.4¢/mile vs ~12¢/mile in a gas car. No system changes required.

🤫

Silent, Fuel-Free

A 32 kWh Panda + Victron inverter is a quieter, lower-maintenance, no-fuel-storage alternative to a propane or natural-gas standby generator — and unlike a generator, it earns money every day it's not in an outage.

🏡

Resale Value Boost

Homes with installed solar and battery storage trade at a premium in Southeast Michigan's increasingly electrification-aware market. Buyers will price in the avoided rate exposure even if they don't compute it themselves.

🌱

Cleaner Energy Mix

Self-consumed solar displaces grid kWh that still includes coal-plant contribution during peak hours. The grid is decarbonizing slowly; your roof can decarbonize faster.

🔧

DIY-Maintainable

48V architecture is below typical shock-hazard voltage. You can add inverters, swap batteries, expand solar, and replace components yourself over the system's 20+ year life. No installer lock-in.

⚙️

Automated by Dynamic ESS

Victron's Dynamic ESS handles the charge/discharge timing automatically against the D1.13 rate schedule. You don't manually toggle the battery — the system optimizes every hour.

Three Levers DTE Customers Can Pull Today

Plan Switch to D1.13

DTE's Overnight Savers plan offers a 11.74¢/kWh super off-peak rate from 1am–7am — the cheapest rate of any DTE plan. Pair it with battery storage and the arbitrage spread to peak (35.56¢) is 23.82¢/kWh today, growing to 40+¢ by 2036.

Battery Arbitrage First

A 32 kWh Docan Panda + Victron inverter setup costs ~$5,865. It pays for itself on rate arbitrage alone — no solar required. Also gives you whole-home backup during outages. Ideal for renters with permission, shaded roofs, or as Stage 1 of a phased build.

Solar Right-Sized for Self-Use

DTE's net-metering replacement pays ~7.75¢ off-peak / ~14¢ peak for exports — far below retail. Size solar to maximize self-consumption, not exports. 8 panels (3.2 kW) covers a typical 19 kWh/day home.

No Federal Tax Credit. Period.

The 30% federal residential solar tax credit (Section 25D) was eliminated effective January 1, 2026 by the One Big Beautiful Bill Act signed July 2025. Every payback calculation here assumes full equipment cost. Third-party owned systems (leases/PPAs) may still qualify under Section 48E commercial credits if construction begins before July 2026 — but homeowners don't directly benefit.
